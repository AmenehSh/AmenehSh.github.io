Ameneh Shamekhi - Paper Requirement

First authored full paper accepted at Persuasive Technology 20171:
Augmenting Group Medical Visits with Conversational Agents for Stress Management Behavior Change Link to the manuscript

Abstract:

Individuals with chronic stress can improve their overall wellness by making lifestyle changes. We describe the design and evaluation of a computer- animated conversational agent that is used in conjunction with Integrative Medical Group Visits (IMGV) to help individuals with chronic pain and depression manage their stress. The agent coaches patients in between group visit sessions, reviewing how to manage stress using non-pharmaceutical techniques (e.g., mindfulness, meditation, yoga self-massage). We conducted a longitudinal clinical trial with 154 participants in which patients in the intervention group interacted with the conversational agent at home on a touch screen tablet to reinforce what they learned in group medical visits. Results from the trial indicate that the conversational agent, in conjunction with medical group visits, leads to significantly more positive stress management behaviors being used by patients after 9 and 21 weeks, compared to patients in usual care.

Statement from Prof. Timothy Bickmore (Adviser)

The paper reports the design of a 21-week intervention our lab developed in collaboration with the Complementary and Alternative Medicine group at Boston Medical Center, along with partial results from a clinical trial conducted there over two years, for patients with chronic pain and depression. Ameneh was the project lead at Northeastern, responsible for collaborating with clinicians at BMC on the design of the intervention, doing the majority of the implementation, supporting BMC through focus group studies and pilot testing, and finally the clinical trial. Ameneh conducted most of the outcome data analyses reported in the paper, wrote the first draft of the manuscript, and led the editing process through to the final submission.
